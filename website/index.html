DESCRIPTION

Particulate Matter, or PM in short is a type of pollutant that is present in the air. PM is a very small class of pollutants and includes all sorts of things such as pollen, dust, smoke, fog and even rain. Being small in diameter, it is measured in Î¼g/m^3. According to this measurement, there are two types of PM values:
PM10: Matter that is 10 micrometers across. Not good for patients with breathing problems, especially asthma.
PM2.5: Matter that is 2.5 micrometers across. Usually consists of gaseous matter, and can be harmful to people with cardiovascular diseases.

Stockholm happens to be polluted by both the above mentioned pollutants from various sources. In particular, the winter tires cause a sharp increase in PM10 values.
